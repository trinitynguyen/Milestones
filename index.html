Nintendo Milestones

Color TV-Game Series (1977-1980)

There are five consoles that are part of the Color TV-Game Series:

  • Color TV-Game 6
  • Color TV-Game 15
  • Color TV-Game Racing 112
  • Color TV-Game Block Breaker
  • Computer TV-Game

Color TV-Game 6 had six different versions of Light Tennis. Each version can have up to 2 players. There was not a detachable controller, but it had sticks attached that players can use to control. It was powered with a white-colored C battery.

Color TV-Game 15 included 2 variations of Hockey, Volleyball and Tennis. It also had an extra game called Penalty Shootout. Each game could have up to a maximum of 2 players. The paddle controls are extended apart from the console and is wired into it.

Color TV-Game Racing 112 is a racing game from a bird’s eye view. The point of the game is to avoid other cars on the road. The consoles implements a steering wheel and gearshift. Another option is to have 2 smaller controllers for multiplayer.

Color TV-Game Block Breaker is a 1-player console that only has 1 game. This game is a version of Block Breaker, a Nintendo arcade game. It has a similar structure as the Color TV-Game 6

Computer TV-Game only had 1 game. It is converted from an arcade game. It can have up to 2 players. It was released exclusively in Japan.

NES (1983)/SNES (1990)

Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) is an 8-bit programmable home console. It is the first home video game console that Nintendo produced. The redesigned NES loads cartridges through a gray covered slot. This design fixed the complications of the original design. The NES comes with a system, controller, and adapter. You connect the adapter to the system then plug it into the T.V.

Super Nintendo Entertainment System (SNES) is a 16-bit home video game console. It is the second programmable home console produced by Nintendo. It is the successor to the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES). It introduced advanced graphics and sound capabilities compared with other systems at that time. It was the best selling console in the 16-bit era. The popularity continued to the 32-bit era. New features were added to the controller. 4 buttons are in a diamond shape and there are 2 shoulder buttons. This console is able to have multiplayer games.

Nintendo 64 (1996)

Nintendo 64 (N64) gets the “64” because of its 64-bit central processing unit. It was the last significant home console to use the cartridge as its main storage format until the latest Nintendo product, the Nintendo Switch. It came in different colors such as gray, green, blue, orange, red black, and pink. The console was on high demand. Even celebrities tried to get their hands on it with special treatment. It discontinued in 2002 and sold almost 33 million units worldwide. In 2015, it was named the 9th best video game console of all time. It was the first Nintendo console to have 3D graphics. The controller is similar to the popular ones many see today. It has ten buttons, one analog stick, and a directional pad. It was the first Nintendo controller to include an analog stick. The controller itself is shaped as an “M”. The best selling game for the N64 was Super Mario 64.

Wii (2006)

The Wii is another one of Nintendo’s home video game consoles. Out of all of Nintendo’s consoles, it is the best selling one. It competed against the Xbox 360 and the PlayStation 3. There were over 101 million units sold around the world. The Wii introduced the Wii controller. The controller is a handheld device with a strap connected to it. The player’s arm would go through the strap to the wrist and tightened. This helps prevent accidentally throwing and breaking the controller. For games, you would insert the Wii game disc into the console. It had a long hollow slit where the player would insert the disc. It allowed multiplayer games with multiple Wii controllers. You control the cursor by pointing the controller towards the console and moving it in the direction you desire. The design is simple and the original is white. However, it comes in many other colors such as black, lime-green, silver, and red.

Nintendo Switch (2017)

The Nintendo Switch is Nintendo’s latest product. It is a hybrid device which means that it is able to be stationary and it is also a portable device. Not only do they get the name “Switch” from the ability to be able to switch from stationary to portable, it also switches the way someone experiences entertainment. The Joy-Con controllers are connected to the console with simple buttons, analog sticks, and directional pads. It connects to the sides of the console for handheld game play. The controllers can be pulled apart from the console and be used for multiplayer purposes. The design is supposed to support playing on the go with friends and playing by yourself at home. Multiplayer is available locally with other Nintendo Switch consoles or online with internet connection. The Nintendo Switch has competitors like the Xbox One and the PlayStation 4. As of the summer of 2019, there have been almost 37 million units sold.
